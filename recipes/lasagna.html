Lasagna

About it

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort dish made with layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable filling, creamy béchamel sauce, and melted cheese. Traditional recipes often include a hearty ragù (slow-cooked meat sauce with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs), layered with velvety béchamel and gooey mozzarella or Parmesan cheese. When baked, the edges become crispy and golden, while the center stays tender and saucy. This hearty, crowd-pleasing meal is perfect for family dinners or gatherings, as it can be prepared ahead of time and reheated without losing flavor.

While lasagna has deep roots in Italian cuisine, many variations exist worldwide. Vegetarian versions might use spinach, mushrooms, or roasted eggplant, while some recipes swap the pasta for zucchini or gluten-free noodles. In the U.S., a quicker "American-style" lasagna often features ricotta cheese instead of béchamel and pre-made marinara sauce for convenience. No matter the recipe, the key to great lasagna lies in balancing the layers—ensuring each bite has the perfect mix of pasta, sauce, cheese, and filling. Serve it with garlic bread and a fresh salad for a complete, satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 9-12 lasagna noodles (or oven-ready/no-boil noodles)
  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (28-oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (15-oz) container ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook noodles (if not oven-ready): Boil in salted water until al dente, then drain and lay flat on a towel.
  3. Make the sauce:
    • Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft.
    • Add ground meat, cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
    • Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 mins.
  4. Prepare cheese mixture: Combine ricotta, egg, parsley, and half the Parmesan in a bowl.
  5. Layer lasagna in a 9x13-inch dish:
    • Spread thin layer of meat sauce on bottom.
    • Add noodles (slightly overlapping), then ricotta mixture, meat sauce, and mozzarella.
    • Repeat layers, ending with noodles, sauce, and remaining cheeses.
  6. Bake covered with foil for 25 mins, then uncovered for 15-20 mins until bubbly and golden.
  7. Rest 10 mins before slicing to set layers.
