Margherita Pizza

About it

Margherita pizza is a classic Neapolitan pizza featuring a thin, chewy crust topped with simple but vibrant ingredients: tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, basil, and a drizzle of olive oil. This pizza celebrates the colors of the Italian flag with its red (tomato), white (mozzarella), and green (basil) toppings. Named after Queen Margherita of Savoy in 1889, this pizza remains a timeless favorite for its perfect balance of flavors and textures.

What makes a great Margherita pizza is the quality of its few ingredients. San Marzano tomatoes, fresh buffalo mozzarella, and hand-torn basil leaves create magic when combined on a perfectly baked crust. While purists insist on wood-fired ovens, you can achieve excellent results at home with a pizza stone or steel. This pizza is ideal for weeknight dinners or entertaining, as it comes together quickly but always impresses.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup San Marzano tomato sauce
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp semolina or cornmeal (for dusting)

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 500°F (260°C) with pizza stone/steel if using.
  2. Prepare dough: Roll/stretch dough to 12-inch circle on floured surface.
  3. Assemble pizza:
    • Transfer dough to parchment/semolina-dusted peel.
    • Spread tomato sauce thinly, leaving 1/2-inch border.
    • Arrange mozzarella slices evenly.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles.
  5. Finish: Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle salt/pepper, and top with fresh basil.
  6. Rest 2 minutes before slicing and serving.
